Week 3: Hand Tools and Fabrication

Kinetic Sculpture

Since my final project revolves around the concept of bubbles, I wanted to take this week as an opportunity to conduct a hands-on experiment. I designed a bubble-blowing mechanism composed of two motors, a fan, and a bubble ring. By separating the control of the fan and the ring, I was able to independently adjust their speeds.

Parameters

Learning from my previous mistake, I made sure to select my laser cutting material before starting the design process. I chose 3.2mm-thick plywood and measured its actual thickness to ensure accuracy. This time, I also assembled the components in Fusion 360 beforehand to simulate the structure and check whether everything would fit together properly.

First Iteration

In the first iteration, I focused on creating a simple prototype to test the bubble-blowing mechanism. I used cardboard to build a basic structure and attached the motors and fan to see how they interacted.



Overall, my first iteration worked functionally. but visually it felt a bit rough. The platform I built turned out larger than necessary, which made the whole setup look bulky and less refined. So in the final version, I reduced the size of the platform.
